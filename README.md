# Investigating Netflix's Revenue Growth Despite Slowing Subscriber Additions (2023–2025)

## Business Context

Netflix is one of the world's largest streaming platforms, serving hundreds of millions of subscribers globally. Historically, subscriber growth has been a major driver of revenue growth for subscription-based businesses.

Between 2023 and 2025, Netflix continued to report strong double-digit revenue growth. However, subscriber growth showed signs of slowing, and Netflix gradually reduced its emphasis on subscriber metrics in external reporting.

Understanding whether Netflix's revenue growth remains dependent on subscriber acquisition or is increasingly driven by other factors is important for evaluating the company's growth strategy and long-term business performance.

---

## Observation

Analysis of Netflix's quarterly financial data revealed that:

* Revenue growth remained strong, ranging from approximately **12.5% to 16.8% year-over-year** between **Q4 2023 and Q1 2025**.
* Subscriber growth showed signs of slowing compared with prior periods.
* Netflix reduced its emphasis on subscriber reporting during 2025 and 2026.
* Despite slower subscriber growth and reduced focus on subscriber metrics, revenue growth remained strong.

These trends suggest that factors beyond subscriber acquisition may have played an increasingly important role in driving Netflix's financial performance.

---

## Business Question

**Why was Netflix able to maintain strong double-digit revenue growth despite slowing subscriber growth between 2023 and 2025?**
